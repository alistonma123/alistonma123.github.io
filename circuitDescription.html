Description

In this short video, the LED circuit lights up with shifting colors and steady pulses. The camera focuses on the breadboard and its connected components, with the Arduino at its center. The LEDs change smoothly between red, green, blue, and blended colors, creating a bright gradient effect.

The changes appear controlled by potentiometers, with each fade and shift following a set pattern. As the lights move, individual LEDs turn on in waves, giving the sense of motion along the strip. The glow also reflects softly on the surrounding surface, adding to the overall look.

The video shows both the clear wiring: jumper cables, resistors, and power lines as well as the visual result of the programming. By keeping the frame close to the circuit, the focus stays on how the code and hardware work together. This recording shows that the project works and that a simple setup can still create an eye-catching effect.
